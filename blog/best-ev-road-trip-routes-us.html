Best EV Road Trip Routes in the U.S.

Published August 17, 2025 • EVMapFinder Blog

Road trips are part of the American experience — and with electric vehicles, they’re becoming more exciting than ever. With the growing EV charging network, you can now explore iconic highways and scenic byways without worrying about running out of charge. Here are some of the best EV road trip routes across the United States, plus tips to plan your journey using EVMapFinder.

1. Pacific Coast Highway (California)

Stretching from Los Angeles to San Francisco and beyond, Highway 1 offers breathtaking ocean views, dramatic cliffs, and dozens of charging stations along the way. DC fast chargers dot the coast, making it ideal for EV travel.

2. Route 66 (Chicago to Santa Monica)

The “Mother Road” runs from Chicago through the Southwest and ends in Santa Monica, CA. Modern EV chargers are spread across major stops, helping you follow this legendary route in sustainable style.

3. Blue Ridge Parkway (Virginia to North Carolina)

Known as “America’s Favorite Drive,” this route spans 469 miles through the Appalachian Mountains. While Level 2 chargers are common in small towns and lodges, plan ahead using EVMapFinder to combine scenic detours with charging stops.

4. I-5 Corridor (Seattle to San Diego)

The entire West Coast is connected via I-5, with dense charging infrastructure from Seattle down to Los Angeles and beyond. Tesla Superchargers and other networks make long-distance travel seamless.

5. Texas Hill Country Loops

Starting from Austin, the Hill Country offers rolling landscapes and winery stops. Public chargers in towns like Fredericksburg make it easy to take a scenic weekend drive.

Tips for Planning EV Road Trips

  • Use EVMapFinder to map out charging stops ahead of time.
  • Check charger speed (DC fast vs Level 2) for accurate timing.
  • Always have a backup station noted in case of outages.
  • Book accommodations with charging included.
